His Excellency Nelson Mandela

The man who brought freedom to South Africa

Timeline of Mandela's life

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, Umtata
  • 1933 - strated his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo.
  • 1939 - Mandela began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare, in Alice, Eastern Cape, He studied English, Anthropology, politics, navtice administration, and Roman Dutch law in his first year, desiring to become an interpreter or clerk in the Native Affairs Department. He helped to found a first-year students' house committee which challenged the dominance of the second-years, and at the end of his first year became involved in a Students' Representative Council (SRC) boycott against the quality of food, for which he was suspended from the university; he never returned to complete his degree.
  • 1941 - Arrived in Johannesburg where he worked as a night watchmam at Crown Mines, his "first sight of South African capitalism in action", he was later fired. He than moved in with a coursin in George Goch Township who introduced him to an ANC activist Walter Sisulu, and he was offered a job as an articled clerk at the law firm of Witkin. To continue his higher education, Mandela signed up to a University of South Africa correspondence course, working on his bachelor's degree at night.
  • 1943 - He got his BA Degree after he passed his BA exams in early 1943,
  • 1943 - Mandela began studying law at the University of the Witwatersrand, and also joined the ANC, and he was increasingly influenced by Sisulu
  • 1944 - With his help and others the African National Congress Youth League (ANCYL) was founded, and Mandela a member of its executive committee. Later that year he got married to Evelyn Mase and they was blessed with their first kid a year later
  • 1947 - Mandela was appointed ANCYL secretary. In December that year he supported an unsuccessful measure to expel communists from the ANC youth league, considering their idealogy un-african because he disagreed with the youth league president's approach. In that same year he was elected to the executive committee of the ANC's Transvaal Province branch, serving under regional president C.S. Ramohanoe. When Ramohanoe acted against the committee, Mandela was one of those who forced his resignation.
  • 1950 - Mandela took Xuma's place on the ANC national executive, and was later elected national president of the ANCYL
  • 1952 - Mandela was arrested and stood trial as one of the 21 accused in Johannesburg. Found guilty of "statutory communism", he was sentence of nine months' hard labour. In December, Mandela was given a six month ban from attending meetings or talking to more than one individual at a time, making his Transvaal ANC presideny impractical.
  • 1953 - Mandela and Tambo opened their own law firm, Mandela and Tambo, operating in downtown Johannesburg. The only African-run law firm in the country, it was popular with aggrieved blacks, often dealing with cases of police brutality.
  • 1956 - Mandela's marriage broke down and Evelyn left him, taking their children to live with her brother. Initiating divorce proceedings in May 1956, she claimed that Mandela had physically abused her; he denied the allegations, and fought for custody of their children. She withdrew her petition of separation in November, but Mandela filed for divorce in January 1958; the divorce was finalised in March, with the children placed in Evelyn's care. During the divorce proceedings, he began courting a social worker, Winnie Madikizela
  • 1956 - In December, Mandela was arrested alongside most of the ANC national executive, and accused of "high treason" against the state. Held in Johannesburg Prision amid mass protests, they underwent a preparatory examination before being granted bail.
  • 1958 - Mandela divorced his wife in March 1958.
  • 1958 - Mandela married Winnie Madikizela. She later became involved in ANC activities, spending several weeks prision. Together they had two children:Zenani, born in February 1959, and Zindziswa (1960 - 2020).
  • 1964 - He was found guilty and sentence to life imprisonment.
  • 1990 - Mandela was released from prision.
  • 1994 - Mandela was inaugrated as the president of South Africa
  • 1999 - Retired from Politics and focused on his philanthropist work.
  • 2004 - "Retiring from retirement" aged 85 and amid failing health, Mandela announced that he was "retiring from retirement" and retreating from public life, remarking, "Don't call me, I will call you."
  • 2011 - Mandela was briefly hospitalised with a respiratory infection.
  • 2013 - died at the age of 95 at his home in Houghton, surrounded by his family.

    • Gracious but steely, [Mandela] steered a country in turmoil toward a negotiated settlement: a country that days before its first democratic election remained violent, riven by divisive views and personalities. He endorsed national reconciliation, an idea he did not merely foster in the abstract, but performed with panache and conviction in reaching out to former adversaries. He initiated an era of hope that, while not long-lasting, was nevertheless decisive, and he garnered the highest international recognition and affection.
